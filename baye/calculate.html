How is Bayes Theorem Calculated?
Let A be one event;
and let B be any other event from the same sample space,
such that P(B) > 0.
Then, Bayes rule can be expressed as:
P(A|B) = (P(A) P(B|A))/ P(B)
where
P(A) is the probability of Event A.
P(B) is the probability of Event B.
P(A|B) is the conditional probability of Event A, given Event B.
P(B|A) is the conditional probability of Event B, given Event A. .

Weather Info

In calculating weather probabilty we have: For example

P(A) "This is the probability that it will rain" Example = 5/365 = 0.0137 [It rains 5 days out of the year.]

P(A') "This is the probability that it will not rain" Example = 360/365 = 0.9863 [It does not rain 360 days out of the year.] (This is automatically calculated)

P(B|A) "This is the probability that the weather man predicts rain and it actually rains" Example = 0.9 [The weatherman predicts rain 90% of the time, when it rains.]

P(B|A') "This is the probability that the weather man predicts rain and it didn't rain" Example = 0.08 [The weatherman predicts rain 8% of the time, when it does not rain.]

Then based on this past data , using bayesian theorem , we will predict the probability that it will rain, given that the weatherman has predicted rain.

Result would be : 0.1351 or 13.51% chance that it will rain.

Disease Info

In calculating disease probabilty we have: for example
Let A be the event that the patient has the illness
Let B be the event that the test result is positive
P(A|B) = probability of the illness given a positive test. This is the number we want to calculate.
P(B|A) = probability of a positive result given illness = 0.92
P(B|A')= probability of a positive result given no illness = 0.10
P(A)= natural probability of the illness = 0.13
Result would be : 0.5789 or 57.89% chance that a person has an illness , given that the test is positive.
